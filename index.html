The Miami Heat (and the Refs) vs. the Atlanta Hawks

An American professional basketball team based in Atlanta. The Hawks compete in the National Basketball Association (NBA) as a member of the league's Eastern Conference Southeast Division. After a tough beginning to the season, the Hawks recovered during the end of the season, as they were able to salvage their playoff hopes as the tenth seed in the conference. They won both of their play-in games and thus became the eighth seed in the conference.

An American professional basketball team based in Miami. The Heat compete in the National Basketball Association (NBA) as a member of the league's Eastern Conference Southeast Division. The Heat breezed through their schedule during the 2022 NBA season, as they were one of the top seeds throughout the entire season. At the end of the season, they rose to the first seed in the East due to injuries on other teams.

"To be honest, the Miami Heat do not have
a very polite way of playing basketball."
- John Collins, Atlanta Hawks

Before the beginning of their matchup, there wass a sense of cautious optimism from Hawks fans. While their team had a rough season, the team had gotten hot when it mattered the most and were playing very well together as a team. Fans were confident that the Hawks could at least put up a fight against the seemingly unbeatble Miami team. However, as game 1 of the series began, it became clear that the Hawks would have to outplay more than just the Heat.

The Miami Heat were mainly the first seed due to their stifiling defense, but in this series, it seems like their defense had crossed the threshold from impressive to dirty. The Hawks players were getting battered around the court like they were playing in the NFL, and the refs were doing nothing to prevent the many plays against the Hawks that could cause major injury.

Though very few Hawks fans expected their favorite team to pull through against one of the clear favorites for the NBA Finals, the clear opinion of most of those same fans is that they would have at least liked the Hawks to have a fair chance. The main appeal to the NBA is the unpredictability of the matchups, but when one team is given more leeway to play rough, then it takes that aspect of the game out of considereation completely.
